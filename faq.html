FAQ

Answers to common questions about intake requests, receiving, prep, storage, pricing, and compliance.

1) How do I start working with PrepNest NY?

Start by submitting the Intake Form with your shipment estimates and required services. We review requests before confirming acceptance and providing receiving instructions.

2) Do you provide your receiving address immediately?

No. The receiving address is provided after request approval to ensure the shipment scope matches our operational capacity and requirements.

3) What happens when my boxes arrive?

All inbound shipments are logged, counted, and visually inspected upon arrival. We note visible issues and report damages or discrepancies when detected.

4) Do you open sealed manufacturer packaging?

We do not open sealed manufacturer packaging unless requested. This helps preserve product integrity and reduces avoidable handling.

5) Which prep services do you offer?

We support standard Amazon FBA prep services including FNSKU labeling, polybagging, bubble wrap, bundling/sets, carton prep, and expiration date labels. Prep is performed according to current Amazon FBA guidelines.

6) Is storage long-term warehousing?

No. Storage is short-term only and intended for temporary holding during prep and outbound scheduling. It is not designed for long-term warehousing.

7) How is storage billed?

Storage is billed per box. Weekly or monthly billing options may apply. Standard box size and weight limits apply.

8) Why do you ask for “units” in the intake form?

Units are used to estimate prep workload and pricing for prep services. Storage billing is per box, not per unit.

9) What files should I upload?

If available, upload your FNSKU labels (PDF). A packing list is optional but recommended for smoother verification and processing.

10) When are outbound shipments released?

Outbound shipments are released only after payment is received. This applies to shipments sent to Amazon FBA.

11) How is pricing determined?

Pricing is based on shipment volume, prep requirements, and storage duration. Prep is billed per unit, receiving per box, and storage per box. Non-standard requests may be quoted individually.

12) Do you have minimums?

Yes. Minimum volume and minimum monthly charges apply.

13) Can a shipment be declined?

Yes. We reserve the right to decline shipments that do not meet our operational or safety requirements.

14) Who is responsible for manufacturer defects or pre-existing damage?

PrepNest NY is not responsible for manufacturer defects, hidden damage, or issues caused prior to receiving inventory. We perform reasonable care and visual inspection upon arrival.

15) Is PrepNest NY affiliated with Amazon?

No. PrepNest NY LLC is an independent service provider and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Amazon.com, Inc. or any of its affiliates.
